How LedgerRocket models For Benefit Of (FBO) accounts, tracks custodial ownership, and maintains FDIC insurance visibility across dual ledgers.

## What Is an FBO Account?

An FBO (For Benefit Of) account is a bank account held by one entity (the custodian) on behalf of another (the beneficial owner). In fintech, the platform operator opens an FBO account at a partner bank to hold customer funds. The bank sees one pooled account; LedgerRocket tracks individual customer positions within it.

Key distinction:

- **Custodian** -- the bank that holds the physical cash
- **Beneficial owner** -- the customer whose money it is

Every account in LedgerRocket carries both an `entity_id` (beneficial owner) and a `custodian_entity_id` (custodian), making the ownership chain explicit.

## Dual-Ledger Pattern

LedgerRocket uses two ledgers to track FBO funds:

### Economic Ledger (Operational)

The on-balance-sheet ledger where business transactions post. Tracks what customers owe and are owed.

- Account codes: 10xxx (house assets), 20xxx (customer liabilities)
- Example: Customer Available (20100), Customer Hold (20101)

### Allocation Ledger (FDIC Tracking)

A parallel ledger that tracks the physical location of funds across bank institutions. Required for FDIC insurance pass-through compliance.

- Account codes: 11xxx (cash balance assets), 21xxx (customer FDIC positions)
- Example: Cash Balances FBO (11900), Consumer FBO Main (21100)

Both ledgers belong to the same entity and currency. Templates fire legs on both ledgers atomically -- every event that moves money on the economic ledger also updates the allocation ledger.

## How Sweeps Move Funds Between Rails

When FBO balances exceed FDIC insurance limits, sweeps move funds to additional bank partners (the "Sweep Network"). Each destination bank is a separate rail.

The sweep lifecycle:

1. **Aggregate** (T5601) -- create clearing positions on source and destination rails
2. **Customer allocation** (T5602) -- per-customer expected movements on the FDIC tracking ledger
3. **Payment initiated** (T5603) -- clear sweep intentions once payment is instructed
4. **Bank statements** (T5604/T5605) -- confirm cash left the FBO and arrived at the sweep bank
5. **Reconciliation** (T5606-T5611) -- flush clearing/control through suspense on both sides

After reconciliation, the allocation ledger reflects the new physical fund locations:

```text
Before sweep:
  Customer A: $250,000 at Master FBO

After sweep:
  Customer A: $150,000 at Master FBO
  Customer A: $100,000 at Sweep Bank 1
```

## FDIC Insurance Tracking

The allocation ledger exists specifically to answer: "At which bank is each customer's money physically located?" This is required for FDIC pass-through insurance, where each customer is insured up to $250,000 per bank.

Querying FDIC positions:

```bash
# Get a customer's FDIC position at the FBO bank
curl -s "https://ledger.dev.ledgerrocket.com/v2/balance-service/api/v1/accounts/CUSTOMER_FBO_MAIN_UUID/balance?site_id=5" \
  -H "x-api-key: lr-test-20250701" | jq .

# Get aggregate FDIC position by account code across all customers
curl -s "https://ledger.dev.ledgerrocket.com/v2/balance-service/api/v1/account-codes/21100/balance?site_id=5" \
  -H "x-api-key: lr-test-20250701" | jq .
```

## Verifying Dual-Ledger Consistency

The economic ledger and allocation ledger must agree. Total customer liabilities on the economic ledger (sum of 20xxx) should equal total customer positions on the allocation ledger (sum of 21xxx).

```bash
# Economic ledger: total customer liabilities
curl -s "https://ledger.dev.ledgerrocket.com/v2/balance-service/api/v1/account-codes/20100/balance?site_id=5" \
  -H "x-api-key: lr-test-20250701" | jq '.credits_posted, .debits_posted'

# Allocation ledger: total FDIC positions
curl -s "https://ledger.dev.ledgerrocket.com/v2/balance-service/api/v1/account-codes/21100/balance?site_id=5" \
  -H "x-api-key: lr-test-20250701" | jq '.credits_posted, .debits_posted'
```

Any discrepancy between these two totals indicates a template configuration issue or an incomplete workflow and should be investigated immediately.
